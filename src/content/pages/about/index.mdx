## How I design and develop

Most of my job titles have included the word "designer", but code is my medium and the browser is my favorite design tool. I believe that designing for the web means working _in the medium_ of the web—namely, HTML and CSS. Interactive media deserves interactive iteration.

I love working digitally, but when I need to think about gnarly problems, I take my sketchbook and my pen, I find a quiet place to sit, and I write.

I try to strike a balance between exploration and execution. During exploration, the goal is to research, learn, and shape an opinion. During execution, the goal is to build things and get them into the world. Too much of the former and you end up drowning in documents without ever shipping something; too much of the latter and you risk solving for problems that don't exist.

## Principles

This is a rough, incomplete, and evolving list of principles that help guide my decision-making:

1. **Do no harm.** How would success affect the flow of resources (time, money, data) from one group to another? How would success affect the environment? If you succeed beyond all expectations, what new problems exist as a result?
2. **Everything is part of a bigger system.** It's [turtles all the way down](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turtles_all_the_way_down). How does a decision within a smaller system conform to (or push back against) a larger system? Is there a way to design "up" a level? To shape the systems that shape systems?
3. **Always be able to articulate "why".** Be wary of tools that prioritize speed over the ability to fully understand the decisions being made. [Embrace friction.](/posts/embrace-friction)
4. **Build on web fundamentals.** When crafting for the web, embrace [progressive enhancement](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Glossary/Progressive_Enhancement) over pixel-perfect consistency. Work with—not against—the fluidity of the medium.
5. **Treat accessibility as a welcome constraint.** You will build more resilient systems as a result. A system that is not accessible is not radical or revolutionary.
6. **Leave it better than you found it.** Clean up messes as you go, even ones you didn't make. Your colleagues will thank you.
7. **Slow progress is powerful.** Think long-term. Longer.
